Design and development of Ampoule packaging system

ROBOTICS AUTOMATION PROJECT

The proposed system involves the utilization of a Six Axis Servo Robot with a 12 kg payload capacity. Its primary function is to efficiently pick up 15 empty glass ampoules simultaneously from a live conveyor operating at a maximum speed of 100 ampoules per minute. Subsequently, these 15 ampoules are meticulously placed inside an empty packing bin or box, facilitated by a "Variable Pitch Electro-Pneumatic Gripper."

The entire process is designed to ensure precision and care in handling the ampoules during the packaging phase. Once the bin or box is filled, an indexer is employed to bring it to the forefront, and the cycle seamlessly continues. Importantly, the cycle time of the entire system is engineered to match the output speed of the conveyor, maintaining a harmonious workflow.

The system's key components include a Six Axis Servo Robot, Variable Pitch Electro-Pneumatic Gripper, live conveyor, and an indexer. The Six Axis Servo Robot, with its 12 kg payload capacity, plays a pivotal role in the swift and accurate picking of 15 ampoules, optimizing the efficiency of the packaging process. The Variable Pitch Electro-Pneumatic Gripper ensures gentle and precise placement of the ampoules within the packing container.

The integration of an indexer ensures the seamless transition of the filled bin or box to the next phase of the process, aligning with the desired production speed. The overarching goal is to achieve a packaging cell with a cycle time precisely synchronized with the conveyor output of 100 ampoules per minute. This project aims to deliver a sophisticated and efficient ampoule packaging system that not only meets the specified requirements but also adheres to industry standards for reliability, speed, and precision.
